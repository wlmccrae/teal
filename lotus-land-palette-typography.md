## Typography

All typefaces are available free on [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com).

### Pairing 1 — DM Serif Display + DM Sans *(Recommended)*

The DM family was designed to work together. DM Serif Display is elegant at large sizes and on dark backgrounds; its italic adds drama to hero lines and pull quotes. DM Sans is exceptionally clean at small sizes, which matters for captions and mobile navigation. Covers the full range from intimate wedding pages to moody concert spreads without switching typefaces.

| Role | Font | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Headings | DM Serif Display | 400 |
| Subheadings | DM Sans | Light 300 |
| Body | DM Sans | Light 300 |
| Navigation / UI | DM Sans | Medium 500 |

---

### Pairing 2 — Fraunces + Outfit *(Warm & distinctive)*

Fraunces is a newer optical-size serif with soft curves that feel warm without being fussy — well suited to the intimate wedding side of the portfolio. Outfit is a rounded geometric sans that keeps things friendly and approachable. Skews slightly warmer and more contemporary than Pairing 1.

| Role | Font | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Headings | Fraunces | Light 300 |
| Body | Outfit | Light 300 |
| Navigation / UI | Outfit | Regular 400 |

---

### Pairing 3 — Libre Baskerville + Inter *(Classic & reliable)*

The most conventional option, but proven. Libre Baskerville renders well at all screen sizes. Inter is among the most legible sans-serifs ever designed for screens. The spaced uppercase navigation treatment gives the site a magazine-like editorial quality. Best choice if you want typography to stay completely out of the way of the photography.

| Role | Font | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Headings | Libre Baskerville | 400 |
| Body | Inter | Light 300 |
| Navigation / UI | Inter | Regular 400, spaced uppercase |

## Usage Notes

- **Dark sections** (near black `#141410`, deep teal-black `#1E2820`) should be used for hero banners, featured portfolio sections, and any area where stage or concert photography appears full-bleed.
- **Light sections** (warm ivory `#FAF8F4`, linen `#F2EDE6`) should carry text-heavy pages: About, Services, Contact, and blog posts.
- **Burnt sienna** (`#A04A2A`) is reserved for primary calls to action only. Avoid using it decoratively or it will lose its signaling function.
- **Scrims** rather than borders should frame photography. Use the dark scrim for neutral overlays and the teal scrim when you want the brand color to show through on a featured image.
- **Category tags** (Weddings, Concerts, Theatre, Dance) use mid teal (`#1D9E75`) as background fill to quietly organize the portfolio without competing with the images.
